## The Train Dilemma

### Introduction

How shall we live as individuals in the face of decisions about right versus wrong? How shall we handle those occasions when none of the choices available to us are clearly good?

### The Classic Dilemma

The trolley problem — pulling a lever to divert a runaway trolley — has been a staple of ethical philosophy for decades. It forces us to confront utilitarian versus deontological reasoning.

### Organizational Application

Leaders regularly face analogous dilemmas: layoffs that save the company but harm individuals, safety disclosures that damage reputation but protect the public. These are real trolley problems.

### Egoism's Answer

Psychological egoism suggests that people always act in their own self-interest, even when appearing altruistic. Moral egoism holds that they should. Both perspectives offer insights into why leaders sometimes make decisions that appear to harm their stakeholders.
