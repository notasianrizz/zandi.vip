## Rise and Fall of SpiceJet

Bryan Zandi – MBA Master's in leadership
Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University

### Overview

Indian aviation has been experiencing a boom over the past decade. SpiceJet, once a promising low-cost carrier, provides a compelling case study in how organizational decision-making driven by short-term thinking and egoistic leadership can undermine long-term viability.

### The Rise

SpiceJet's early years were marked by aggressive growth and market capture. The airline disrupted the Indian aviation market with competitive pricing and rapid fleet expansion.

### Environmental Scanning

A PESTEL analysis of SpiceJet's operating environment reveals significant external pressures: volatile fuel prices, regulatory challenges, intense competition from IndiGo, and the economic disruption of the COVID-19 pandemic.

### The Fall

Leadership decisions prioritizing short-term gains over sustainable operations led to financial difficulties. Delayed salary payments, grounded aircraft, and regulatory scrutiny followed.

### Lessons Learned

The SpiceJet case illustrates how organizational egoism — prioritizing the interests of leadership over stakeholders — creates fragile organizations ill-equipped to weather adversity.
