## Theories of Ethics

### Overview

Ethical decision-making is at the heart of effective leadership. Understanding the major theories of ethics equips leaders with multiple lenses through which to evaluate complex situations.

### Utilitarianism

Utilitarian ethics holds that the right action maximizes overall well-being. Leaders applying utilitarian reasoning ask: "What decision produces the greatest good for the greatest number?"

### Deontological Ethics

Kantian ethics emphasizes duty and universal principles. Certain actions are inherently right or wrong regardless of their consequences. A deontological leader asks: "Could I will this action to be a universal law?"

### Virtue Ethics

Virtue ethics focuses on character rather than rules or outcomes. The virtuous leader asks: "What would a person of excellent character do?"

### Egoism

Ethical egoism holds that individuals ought to act in their own self-interest. While often contrasted with altruistic ethics, rational self-interest can align with broader organizational goals when structured correctly.

### Application

The most effective leaders draw from multiple ethical frameworks, adapting their reasoning to the specific context and stakeholders involved.
